Xchurch/Xtchurch¶

n. Christchurch [Xchurch usual in Phillips' Journal (also Xch)]

June BUTLER I saw her standing near the market place in XtChurch the day before (1860,Forest Creek MS,43) [NOTES: 1960 ed.]

PHILLIPS H.P. & Leech went to Aylmers on the road to Xchurch. Ibid. 1856 16 Dec 1959 Leach called on his way to Xch. (Aug 1, 1855,Journal Kept at Rockwood and the Point,5)

Xchurch has grown and makes a good deal of show from the Port Hills. (Aug 1, 1962,Richmond-Atkinson Papers,1/803,1960 ed.) [NOTES: (C.W. Richmond)]

XYZ¶

abbr.

McGILL XYZ the YMCA, ironically displaced acronym, WWI soldiers. (1968,Dictionary of Kiwi Slang, A,127)
